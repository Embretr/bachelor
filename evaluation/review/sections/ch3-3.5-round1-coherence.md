### 1. §3.5.5 ¶Tried — three-button compression of the four-action lock (fixable)
**Quote (line ~59):** "an explicit accept / modify / reject control on every algorithm-generated suggestion"
**Problem:** The four-action lock from CLAUDE.md and the spine is "inspect, modify, accept, or reject" (four actions). The opening framing of §3.5 (line 67) and the §3.1/§3.2 origin paragraphs both use the full four-action formula. §3.5.5 ¶Tried compresses to three buttons (accept / modify / reject), dropping "inspect". Defensible as a literal UI-control description (inspection is the display prerequisite, not a button), but it weakens the visibility of the four-action lock at the exact iteration where it matters most.
**Fix:** (a) "an explicit inspect / modify / accept / reject control on every algorithm-generated suggestion" so all four canonical verbs appear; or (b) "with inspection of every suggestion as the precondition that the timeline view continuously delivers".
**Rule:** Anchor Concept Coherence — "accountable to the traffic coordinator" must be operationalised as inspect, modify, accept, or reject (CLAUDE.md §5).
