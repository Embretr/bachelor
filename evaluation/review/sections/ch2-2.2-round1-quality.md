## Chapter-2-specific question — comparison and limitations

**Comparisons across sources — present:**

- ¶5 names the relationship between Lee's misuse/disuse and the rest of the layered theory: Lee provides the older foundation; Bainbridge supplies the structural asymmetry, Hoff & Bashir the calibration empirics, Miller the explanation requirement.
- ¶4 implicitly relates Hoff & Bashir's calibration to Miller's explanation by making explanation a precondition for calibration ("Calibration depends on the coordinator being able to interpret what the algorithm has done").
- ¶3 + ¶5 together connect early-error trust degradation (Hoff & Bashir) to misuse/disuse (Lee).

**Comparisons not made:**

- The level-5/6 boundary is named as a design choice but not theoretically critiqued. Parasuraman's claim that "appropriate LOA is bounded — lower and upper limit" (notes p. 290/PDF 5) could let the section explicitly say *why* level 5 falls inside the bound for this domain (not just inside the team's comfort zone). This is a minor missed opportunity rather than a deficiency.
- Hoff & Bashir's findings are aggregate (127 studies, primarily aviation/military lab settings); this aggregate-to-small-coordinator-pool transfer is not explicitly flagged. The notes' "forbehold" (`hoff2015trust.md`) names the domain-mismatch risk; surfacing it in ¶3 would be A+. Minor suggestion only.

**Project-specific application — present:** ¶6 names six concrete artefact mechanisms (Gantt timeline, summary panel, deviation viewer, drag-to-reassign, soft-warning-not-blocking, per-company weights, engine/time-budget exposure) and ties each to a theoretical layer.
