# Sensurveiledning — Bacheloroppgaver i Ingeniørfag

> **Source:** UHR-NRT (Nasjonalt råd for teknologisk utdanning) — Karakterbeskrivelser for sensur av bacheloroppgaver i ingeniørfag, etter rammeplan av 3. februar 2011.
> **Purpose:** This is the official grading rubric. The quality agent reads this when evaluating chapters. The writer agent reads this to understand what "A-level" means concretely.
> **Note:** "Arbeidet" = the written thesis + any product + any oral presentation.

---

## Grade Descriptors

### A — Fremragende (Outstanding)

1. **Overall:** Outstanding performance that clearly stands out.
2. **Engineering insight:** Very strong engineering insight and subject knowledge at a very high level.
3. **Theory and method:** Selects and applies relevant theory and methods in a very convincing manner.
4. **Execution:** Very relevant and clear problem statement. Plans and executes engineering work at very high quality.
5. **Results:** Work appears advanced and/or innovative. Analysis and discussion are very well grounded, well reasoned, and clearly linked to the problem statement. Very strong ability to reflect. Clearly distinguishes own contributions from others'.
6. **Presentation:** Form, communication, structure, and language at a very high level.

### B — Meget god (Very good)

1. **Overall:** Very good performance.
2. **Engineering insight:** Very good engineering insight and subject knowledge at a high level.
3. **Theory and method:** Selects and applies relevant theory and methods in a very convincing manner.
4. **Execution:** Very relevant and clear problem statement. Plans and executes engineering work at very high quality.
5. **Results:** Work appears very good and/or innovative. Analysis and discussion are very well grounded, well reasoned, and clearly linked to the problem statement. Very good ability to reflect. Clearly distinguishes own contributions from others'.
6. **Presentation:** Form, communication, structure, and language at a very high level.

### C — God (Good)

1. **Overall:** Good performance.
2. **Engineering insight:** Good engineering insight and good subject knowledge.
3. **Theory and method:** Selects and applies relevant theory and methods in a good manner.
4. **Execution:** Relevant and mainly clear problem statement. Plans and executes engineering work at good quality.
5. **Results:** Work appears good with elements of creativity. Analysis and discussion are well grounded and linked to the problem statement. Good ability to reflect. Mostly distinguishes clearly between own contributions and others'.
6. **Presentation:** Form, communication, structure, and language at a good level.

### D — Nokså god (Fairly good)

1. **Overall:** Clearly acceptable performance.
2. **Engineering insight:** Fairly good engineering insight and fairly good subject knowledge.
3. **Theory and method:** Can mostly apply relevant theory and methods.
4. **Execution:** Mainly relevant and clear problem statement, but goals may be somewhat unclear. Planning and execution at a clearly acceptable level.
5. **Results:** Work appears fairly good. Analysis and discussion are well grounded and linked to the problem statement, but with potential for improvement. Shows ability to reflect but may have problems clearly distinguishing own contributions from others'.
6. **Presentation:** Form, communication, structure, and language at an acceptable level.

### E — Tilstrekkelig (Sufficient)

1. **Overall:** Acceptable performance that satisfies minimum requirements.
2. **Engineering insight:** Sufficient engineering insight and sufficient subject knowledge.
3. **Theory and method:** Can to some extent apply relevant theory and methods.
4. **Execution:** Sufficiently clear problem statement, but goals are described unclearly. Planning and execution at an acceptable level, but candidate shows limited independent progression.
5. **Results:** Work appears relatively modest and somewhat fragmented. Analysis and discussion are sufficiently grounded but should have been better linked to the problem statement. Shows necessary ability to reflect but may have problems clearly distinguishing own contributions from others'.
6. **Presentation:** Mostly acceptable but with noticeable deficiencies in form, communication, structure, and language.

### F — Ikke bestått (Fail)

1. **Overall:** Performance that does not satisfy minimum requirements.
2. **Engineering insight:** Does not have necessary engineering insight, insufficient subject knowledge.
3. **Theory and method:** Shows lacking competence to apply relevant theory and methods.
4. **Execution:** Cannot formulate a sufficiently clear problem statement. Goals are unclear or not described. Planning and execution are not acceptable.
5. **Results:** Work appears modest and fragmented. Analysis and discussion are not sufficiently grounded and loosely linked to the problem statement. Does not show necessary ability to reflect. Little distinction between own contributions and others'.
6. **Presentation:** Significant deficiencies in form, communication, structure, and language.

## Overall Weighting: Product / Report / Process

Before the six NRT criteria are applied, the thesis is assessed across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Default weight (development project) | What it covers |
|-----------|:------------------------------------:|----------------|
| **Product** | **40%** | The software system, including system documentation |
| **Report** | **40%** | The main report with appendices (vision doc, requirements doc count here) |
| **Process** | **20%** | Project execution, method implementation, timelists, meeting minutes, plan vs. reality |

This means the system itself counts as much as the written thesis. For more theoretical projects, Report goes up to 80% and Product drops.

## The Six Assessment Criteria — Detailed

These are the six dimensions sensors evaluate, with their sub-questions.

### 1. Generelt inntrykk (General impression) — Weight: 10–15%

| Sub-criterion | What the sensor asks |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Overall impression** | What is the holistic impression of the work? |
| **Independence** | To what degree has the candidate independently generated important elements, problem statements, and ideas? Can the candidate independently find and use relevant literature and methods? Is there evidence of personal initiative? What type of help and guidance was received? |
| **Level** | Is the work assessed at the appropriate level for a bachelor's degree in engineering? |
| **Timeliness** | Was the work delivered within the normative timeframe? |

### 2. Ingeniørfaglig innsikt (Engineering insight) — Weight: 15–25%

What the sensor asks:
- Is the engineering foundation well described?
- Is the work placed in a holistic system perspective — showing lifecycle, environmental, health, societal, economic, and ethical perspectives?
- Can the candidate update their knowledge through information retrieval and contact with professional communities?

**Implication for Ressursplanlegger:** The thesis must show awareness of the transport domain beyond just the software — including working conditions, environmental impact (sustainability section), economic implications, and ethical considerations around automation replacing human judgment.

**NTNU-specific note:** A subsection on "Yrkesetisk refleksjon – samfunnsnytte" (professional ethics and societal benefit) in the main report counts directly toward this criterion. This is the sustainability/ethics section in Chapter 5.

### 3. Teoretisk innsikt (Theoretical insight) — Weight: 15–25%

What the sensor asks:
- Does the work document good theoretical overview and specialisation in the engineering field?
- Does it demonstrate knowledge of relevant research, development, methods, and working approaches?

**Implication for Ressursplanlegger:** Chapter 2 must demonstrate real understanding of resource scheduling, constraint programming, HITL design, and DSR methodology — not just summaries but showing how the theories connect to and inform the system design.

### 4. Gjennomføring (Execution) — Weight: 15–25%

| Sub-criterion | What the sensor asks |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Problem statement** | Is the problem statement with background and goals presented clearly and understandably? |
| **Skill level** | Does the work demonstrate ability to plan and execute an engineering project? Does it demonstrate ability to find, evaluate, use, and reference information and subject matter to illuminate a problem statement? |

**Implication for Ressursplanlegger:** Chapter 1 must have a crystal-clear research question. Chapter 3 must show methodical, well-planned execution of both user research and system development.

### 5. Resultat (Results) — Weight: 15–25%

| Sub-criterion | What the sensor asks |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **The result** | Does the work build on prior research and development? Does it show quality, creativity, and contribute to innovation or realisation of sustainable and societally useful products/systems/solutions? |
| **Analysis and discussion** | Is the analysis and discussion well grounded, well reasoned, and clearly linked to the problem statement? Is the evaluation of results built on a methodical approach? |
| **Reflection** | Is there a reasonable assessment of the significance of the results? Does the candidate relate critically to different information sources? Are uncertainties (method errors, measurement errors, etc.) evaluated and discussed? Are relevant professional, vocational, societal, and research-ethical issues analysed? |
| **Own contribution / goal achievement** | Can the candidate clearly distinguish own contribution from others' (sources and clear references)? Does the conclusion give a good presentation of the degree to which goals were achieved? Is there a sensible and reasoned proposal for future work? |

**NTNU-specific note:** Three types of results are assessed:
- *Engineering results:* Does the product meet the goals in vision/requirements documents? Deviations discussed?
- *Scientific/knowledge-based results:* Is the research question from Chapter 1 answered?
- *Administrative results:* Goal achievement relative to the progress plan.

**Implication for Ressursplanlegger:** This is the highest-impact criterion. The thesis must:
- Clearly link analysis/discussion back to the research question at every step
- Show critical reflection on limitations (not just list them)
- Distinguish clearly what Mikael did (user research, requirements) vs. Embret did (development, algorithm) vs. what the system does
- Include a reasoned future work section

### 6. Fremstilling (Presentation) — Weight: 10–15%

| Sub-criterion | What the sensor asks |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Structure** | Does the written work have a structured and logical composition? Is it generally clear? Is there a consistent style for references, figures, and tables? |
| **Form and communication** | Are the problem statement and results communicated with necessary professional and linguistic precision? Is the report readable with good linguistic quality? What is the quality of figures and tables? |

**Implication for Ressursplanlegger:** Consistent LaTeX formatting, proper use of `booktabs` tables, labelled figures, consistent citation style (APA 7 / biblatex), and clear academic English throughout.

## What This Means for Achieving an A

To score 90+ points, the thesis must score "very high" on ALL six criteria. Concretely:

1. **General impression:** The thesis feels polished, professional, and clearly the work of candidates who took initiative and worked independently.
2. **Engineering insight (highest weight at 25%):** The work is placed in a holistic system perspective. Sustainability, societal impact, and ethical considerations are not afterthoughts — they are woven in.
3. **Theoretical insight:** Theory chapter demonstrates genuine understanding, not just summaries. Theories are used in analysis and discussion, not just presented.
4. **Execution:** Research question is crisp. Method is well-justified. The development process is documented and traceable.
5. **Results:** Analysis is deep, not surface-level. Discussion weighs multiple perspectives. Reflection is genuine and specific. Own contributions are crystal clear.
6. **Presentation:** Consistent, professional formatting. Clear academic language. High-quality figures and tables.

**The single biggest differentiator between A and B:** The quality of analysis, discussion, and critical reflection in Criterion 5. A B-grade thesis presents good results. An A-grade thesis *interrogates* its results — asking "what does this mean?", "what are the limitations?", "what would we do differently?", and "how does this relate to the broader field?"
