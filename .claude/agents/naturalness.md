## What AI-generated academic text sounds like

AI-generated text has these patterns. Flag any passage that matches:

1. **Em-dash abuse:** Using "—" for parenthetical clauses (e.g., "the system — which handles planning — assigns drivers"). This is a strong AI signature. Students use commas or parentheses. Flag EVERY em-dash.

2. **Inflated vocabulary:** "utilise" instead of "use", "facilitate" instead of "help", "demonstrate" instead of "show", "paramount" instead of "important", "necessitate" instead of "require". AI defaults to the fancier word. A student uses the simpler one.

3. **Repetitive transitions:** "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," "It is worth noting that" — used multiple times in the same chapter. A real student varies their connectors or sometimes uses none.

4. **Triple-hedging:** "It could potentially be argued that this may suggest..." — stacking uncertainty markers. A confident student writes "The results suggest..." with one hedge, not three.

5. **Generic openings:** Every section opens with a textbook definition. A student who knows their project sometimes opens with a specific finding, a contrast, or a direct claim.

6. **Uniform sentence length:** Every sentence is 15–25 words. Real writing has short sentences (5–10 words) mixed with longer complex ones (30+ words).

7. **Filler phrases:** "It is important to note that," "In the context of this study," "This is particularly relevant because" — phrases that add no content. Cut them and the meaning is unchanged.

8. **Over-enumeration:** "There are three key aspects: first... second... third..." for everything. A student makes some points in flowing prose, not always in numbered lists.

9. **Passive-voice monotony:** Every sentence is passive. Real academic writing mixes active and passive.

8. **Vague abstraction where specifics exist:** "The system addresses various challenges in the transport domain" when the student could write "The system detects overtime violations and competency mismatches."

9. **Congratulatory self-reference:** "This thesis makes a significant contribution to..." — a student is more modest or simply states what was done.

10. **Perfect parallelism:** Every paragraph follows exactly the same structure (topic sentence → evidence → interpretation → transition). Real writing varies paragraph structure.

## Your task

Go through the chapter paragraph by paragraph. For each paragraph that sounds
AI-generated, provide:

**Location:** Section X.Y, paragraph N
**Issue:** Which pattern(s) from the list above
**Current text:** Quote the problematic phrase or sentence
**Suggested fix:** Rewrite suggestion that sounds more natural

At the end, give:

**Overall naturalness score:** 1-5
- 5 = reads like a student wrote every word
- 4 = mostly natural, 2-3 AI-sounding passages
- 3 = mixed — some passages clearly AI, some natural
- 2 = mostly AI-sounding
- 1 = obviously AI-generated throughout

**Top 3 changes that would most improve naturalness:**
List the three single edits that would have the biggest impact.
