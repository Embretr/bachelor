# Thesis Outline (Disposisjon)

> Update this as the structure evolves. Claude Code reads this before writing any chapter.

---

## Chapter 1 — Introduction

**Purpose:** Orient the reader, establish why the problem matters, and state what the thesis does.

**Sections:**
- 1.1 Background and Motivation
- 1.2 Research Questions
- 1.3 Scope and Delimitations
- 1.4 Thesis Structure

**Target length:** ~3–5 pages

---

## Chapter 2 — Theory / Literature Review

**Purpose:** Present the theoretical foundation and position the thesis in existing research.

**Sections:**
- 2.1 [Topic area 1]
- 2.2 [Topic area 2]
- 2.3 Related Work

**Target length:** ~8–12 pages

---

## Chapter 3 — Methodology

**Purpose:** Explain how the research was conducted and justify the choices made.

**Sections:**
- 3.1 Research Design
- 3.2 Data Collection
- 3.3 Data Analysis
- 3.4 Validity and Reliability

**Target length:** ~5–8 pages

---

## Chapter 4 — Findings / Results

**Purpose:** Present what was found — without interpretation.

**Sections:**
- 4.1 [Finding area 1]
- 4.2 [Finding area 2]

**Target length:** ~8–12 pages

---

## Chapter 5 — Discussion

**Purpose:** Interpret findings in light of theory and research questions.

**Sections:**
- 5.1 Interpretation of Findings
- 5.2 Implications
- 5.3 Limitations

**Target length:** ~6–10 pages

---

## Chapter 6 — Conclusion

**Purpose:** Summarise, answer the research questions, and suggest future work.

**Sections:**
- 6.1 Summary
- 6.2 Answers to Research Questions
- 6.3 Future Work

**Target length:** ~2–4 pages
