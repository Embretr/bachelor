## Sammendrag (2–3 setninger)

Malterud (2001) proposes **three** overall standards for scientific quality in qualitative research — relevance, validity, and reflexivity — and elaborates specific challenges around reflexivity (researcher positionality), transferability (purposive sampling and external validity), and interpretation/analysis (transparent procedure from data to findings). The article is written for medical researchers encountering qualitative methods and includes a practical guidelines checklist (Panel 3) for authors and reviewers. Its primary contribution to this thesis is the validity framework for §3.7: justifying purposive sampling of seven coordinators, defending small sample size, framing declared researcher preconceptions as legitimate rather than biasing, and applying reflexivity as a quality criterion to the dev-team-as-research-team configuration.

## Claims this source supports

### Claim: "Three standards govern scientific quality in qualitative research: relevance, validity, and reflexivity"
- **Suggested for:** Ch 3.7 ¶1 (primary — the cited paragraph for Malterud in the outline)
- **Direkte sitat:** "I believe relevance and validity are essential standards, but think of reflexivity as an equally important measure, which should be added to the criteria." (p. 483 / PDF 1)
- **Parafrase:** Qualitative research must meet three standards — relevance (is the research question worth answering?), validity (does the study investigate what it claims?), and reflexivity (is the researcher's effect on the process acknowledged and managed?).
- **Forbehold:** The article's abstract uses different phrasing ("I propose relevance, validity, and reflexivity as overall standards") — both are on p. 483. The standard count is **three**, not four. The outline's claim of "four criteria — systematic critical reflection, relevance, validity, reflexivity" does NOT match this source (see Forbehold og begrensninger below).
- **Anvendelse på vårt case:** For §3.7, the thesis applies these three criteria to its own methodology: relevance (resource-utilization visibility gap in Norwegian transport is a practically relevant problem), validity (purposive sample across 7 companies, triangulation across interviews, requirements traceability as system validation), and reflexivity (dev team = research team + Admmit affiliation are declared preconceptions, not denied).

### Claim: "Reflexivity requires identifying and declaring preconceptions — preconceptions are not bias unless undisclosed"
- **Suggested for:** Ch 3.7 ¶4 (researcher bias paragraph); Ch 5.4 L3 (author affiliation with Admmit)
- **Direkte sitat:** "Preconceptions are not the same as bias, unless the researcher fails to mention them." (p. 484 / PDF 2)
- **Parafrase:** The researcher's prior beliefs, professional background, and hypotheses become bias only when concealed; disclosed preconceptions are valid sources of relevant research focus.
- **Forbehold:** Written for medical researchers; the principle generalises to IS research but the specific domain examples (GP distress, patient angina) are medical.
- **Anvendelse på vårt case:** The thesis research team is simultaneously the development team and has an Admmit affiliation (L3). Malterud's framework transforms this from a disqualifying conflict into a declared positionality: as long as §3.7 names the author's prior beliefs (hypothesis about the visibility gap, Admmit's role in shaping requirements), reflexivity is maintained rather than violated. This is the standard academic move for researcher-practitioner studies.

### Claim: "Small purposive samples can be adequate for qualitative inquiry; sample size is determined by the research question, not a fixed number"
- **Suggested for:** Ch 3.7 ¶2 (interview validity); Ch 5.4 L1 defence
- **Direkte sitat:** "The nature of the research question and the material, combined with the intention of external validity, will determine the correct number of participants for a study. One individual, as in a case study, might be sufficient dependent on the topic and scope of the investigation." (p. 486 / PDF 4)
- **Parafrase:** There is no minimum threshold; adequacy is judged against the research question, not a head-count norm.
- **Forbehold:** Malterud's examples are from medical GP research (15 patients + 4 doctors). The principle is presented as general qualitative methodology, but the thesis must still acknowledge that 7 coordinators is limited (L1).
- **Anvendelse på vårt case:** Seven traffic coordinators from companies of varying size and system maturity constitutes a purposive sample adequate for surfacing patterns around the visibility gap — the research question is about coordinator experience, not prevalence estimation. Malterud's framing supports the §3.7 / §5.4 L1 argument: the limitation is acknowledged, but does not invalidate the findings as descriptions applicable within a specified setting.

### Claim: "Purposive (or theoretical) sampling — not random sampling — is the appropriate strategy for qualitative inquiry"
- **Suggested for:** Ch 3.3 ¶2 (participant selection rationale); Ch 3.7 ¶2
- **Direkte sitat:** "Purposeful or theoretical sampling are commonly done to obtain qualitative material." (p. 485 / PDF 3)
- **Parafrase:** Unlike quantitative research requiring representative random samples for statistical inference, qualitative research selects participants to maximise variation or theoretical relevance.
- **Forbehold:** Malterud contrasts purposive sampling with random sampling but does not define purposive sampling in detail; Kvale (also cited in thesis) provides more operational guidance.
- **Anvendelse på vårt case:** The seven coordinators were selected purposively for geographic and company-size variation, not randomly. Malterud provides the methodological justification for this choice in §3.3 ¶2.

---

### Claim: "Transferability (external validity) is not universal applicability but applicability within a specified setting"
- **Suggested for:** Ch 3.7 ¶2; Ch 5.4 L11 (single-domain)
- **Direkte sitat:** "The findings from a qualitative study are not thought of as facts that are applicable to the population at large, but rather as descriptions, notions, or theories applicable within a specified setting." (p. 486 / PDF 4)
- **Parafrase:** The goal is not generalisability to all populations but bounded transferability — the reader must be given enough contextual information to judge applicability to their own setting.
- **Forbehold:** Malterud's framing is medical qualitative research. The principle is widely accepted across qualitative traditions (Lincoln & Guba, Mays & Pope) so transfer to IS research is unproblematic.
- **Anvendelse på vårt case:** The thesis's interview findings apply to Norwegian transport companies fitting the described profile (SME, coordinator-operated dispatch, similar TMS landscape). Claims of broader applicability (other countries, other industries) fall outside the bounded transferability — which L11 already acknowledges.

## Forbehold og begrensninger

- **Critical: outline's "four criteria" claim does NOT match this source.** The paper proposes **three** standards: relevance, validity, reflexivity. The outline (§3.7 ¶1) says "Malterud's four criteria — systematic critical reflection, relevance, validity, reflexivity." There is no fourth criterion called "systematic critical reflection" in this paper. The closest textual basis is the statement that qualitative research is "a systematic and reflective process" (p. 483), but this is a description of qualitative research generally, not a separate criterion. The writer must either cite only three criteria verbatim from the source, or find a different source for a fourth. Do not invent a fourth criterion attributed to this source.
- **Medical domain, not IS/software.** All examples and discussions refer to medical research (general practice, patient interviews). The criteria generalise to qualitative inquiry broadly (Malterud explicitly claims this), but specific procedural guidance (Giorgi analysis, clinical knowledge) is not transferable to this thesis.
- **Thematic analysis not discussed.** The thesis uses thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, cited in §3.4). Malterud discusses grounded theory, Giorgi's phenomenological analysis, and narrative analysis, but not Braun & Clarke. Cite Malterud for the validity framework only; cite Braun & Clarke for the analysis procedure.
- **No discussion of mixed methods in the DSR sense.** Malterud's treatment of combining qualitative and quantitative is about sequential or triangulated study designs, not about DSR's artefact-plus-knowledge contribution structure. Do not use Malterud to justify the DSR choice (Hevner covers that).
- **Reflexivity is presented as individual researcher's responsibility.** Malterud discusses single researcher and multiple researcher scenarios, but not researcher-as-developer scenarios specific to IS artefact development. The application to dev-team-as-research-team is a domain bridge the writer must make explicit.

## Rammeverk og modeller

### Three standards for qualitative inquiry (p. 483)

| Standard | Hva det er | Kildens operasjonalisering |
|---|---|---|
| Relevance | Er forskningsspørsmålet et relevant problem? | Detailed reports; sampling techniques that match the question |
| Validity | Undersøker studien det den hevder å undersøke? | Triangulation, respondent validation, clear methods description, attention to negative cases, fair dealing |
| Reflexivity | Er forskerens effekt på prosessen erkjent og håndtert? | Declare preconceptions before start; establish metapositions; look for competing conclusions |

### Panel 2: Factors that affect research (p. 484)

| Faktor | Prosedyre |
|---|---|
| Reflexivity | Share preconceptions; establish metapositions |
| Transferability | Adequate and sufficiently varied sample; consider whom and what the findings concern |

### Guidelines checklist (Panel 3, p. 485)

Sections: Aim, Reflexivity, Method and design, Data collection and sampling, Theoretical framework, Analysis, Findings, Discussion, Presentation, References. Each section has 2–5 yes/no questions for authors and reviewers of qualitative studies. This checklist is a practical application of the three standards framework and can be used as a self-audit for §3.7 and §5.6.

## Key arguments / lines of reasoning

### Argument: Reflexivity transforms bias into valid positionality
- **Premiss:** Every researcher enters with preconceptions; these cannot be eliminated.
- **Premiss:** "Subjectivity arises when the effect of the researcher is ignored."
- **Konklusjon:** If preconceptions are declared and their effect assessed at every step, they become a resource for relevant research rather than a source of contamination.
- **Sted:** (pp. 483–484 / PDF 1–2)
- **Hvilke claims dette støtter:** Ch 3.7 ¶4 (researcher bias); Ch 5.4 L3 (author affiliation with Admmit)

### Argument: Qualitative findings are not facts but bounded descriptions
- **Premiss:** Qualitative material uses non-representative purposive samples; statistical inference cannot be applied.
- **Premiss:** External validity in qualitative research = applicability to specified settings, not populations.
- **Konklusjon:** Qualitative studies are valid when they produce "descriptions, notions, or theories applicable within a specified setting."
- **Sted:** (pp. 485–486 / PDF 3–4)
- **Hvilke claims dette støtter:** Ch 3.7 ¶2; Ch 5.4 L1 (sample size defence), L11 (single-domain)
