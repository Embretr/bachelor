## Sammendrag (2–3 setninger)

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development is a UN General Assembly resolution adopted by 193 member states that commits to 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and 169 associated targets by the year 2030. The agenda balances economic, social, and environmental dimensions of sustainable development, addressing areas including decent work, resource efficiency, sustainable industry and infrastructure, and sustainable transport. It functions as a global normative framework used across disciplines to anchor sustainability-oriented design and policy claims.

## Key arguments / lines of reasoning

### Argument: SDGs as universal normative reference for sustainability analysis

- **Premiss(er):** The SDGs are adopted by all UN member states and are "universal goals and targets which involve the entire world, developed and developing countries alike" (p. 3/35); they address economic, social, and environmental sustainability in an integrated manner.
- **Konklusjon:** Any technology intervention can be evaluated against the SDG framework as a normative reference — not to claim policy compliance, but to structure a sustainability analysis using an internationally recognised taxonomy.
- **Sted:** pp. 1–3/35 (Preamble and Declaration introduction)
- **Hvilke claims dette støtter:** Ch 5.3 §5.3 ¶4

### Argument: Resource efficiency is simultaneously an economic and environmental goal

- **Premiss(er):** SDG 8.4 links resource efficiency in production to decoupling economic growth from environmental degradation; SDG 9.4 frames resource-use efficiency as a component of sustainable industrialization.
- **Konklusjon:** A system that improves resource utilization (reducing overtime, idle time, uneven load) can be positioned as contributing to both economic efficiency and reduced environmental footprint — the two dimensions are linked by the SDG framework itself.
- **Sted:** pp. 19–20/35 (SDG 8.4 and SDG 9.4 targets)
- **Hvilke claims dette støtter:** Ch 5.3 §5.3 ¶2 (sustainability benefits table, resource-efficiency row)

### Argument: Decent work and safe working conditions are SDG-protected interests, creating a normative basis for the working-conditions dilemma

- **Premiss(er):** SDG 8.5 targets full and productive employment with equal pay; SDG 8.8 targets labour rights and safe working environments for all workers.
- **Konklusjon:** Algorithmic optimization of driver schedules operates in a space where SDG 8-protected interests are at stake — creating a normative basis for the ethical concern that optimization must not come at the cost of driver working conditions.
- **Sted:** pp. 19–20/35 (SDG 8.5 and 8.8 targets)
